Year 1 Basics

Core Definitions & Network Theorems

Fundamental Circuit Quantities

Electric Charge (Q): Fundamental property of matter measured in Coulombs ($C$). $1\,\text{C} = 6.24 \times 10^{18}$ electrons.

Current (I): Time rate of flow of electric charge: $I = \frac{dQ}{dt}$ (Amperes).

Voltage (V): Energy required to move a unit positive charge between two nodes: $V = \frac{dW}{dQ}$ (Volts).

Power (P) & Energy (W): $P = V \cdot I = \frac{dW}{dt}$ (Watts). $W = \int P \, dt$ (Joules).

Independent vs. Dependent Sources

  • Independent Voltage/Current Source: Delivers terminal voltage or current completely independent of external network variables.
  • Dependent (Controlled) Sources: Output value is governed by a voltage or current elsewhere in the circuit (VCVS, VCCS, CCVS, CCCS).

Core Network Theorems

Thevenin's Theorem: Any linear two-terminal bilateral network can be replaced by an equivalent circuit consisting of an open-circuit voltage $V_{th}$ in series with an equivalent resistance $R_{th}$.

Norton's Theorem: Dual of Thevenin's theorem; replaces a linear network with a short-circuit current source $I_n$ in parallel with $R_n$ ($R_n = R_{th}$).

Maximum Power Transfer Theorem: Maximum power is transferred from source to load when load resistance equals internal source resistance ($R_L = R_{s}$ for DC, or $Z_L = Z_s^*$ for AC).

Analog Unit I

Semiconductor Diodes & Wave Shaping

Volt-Ampere (V-I) Characteristics & Shockley Equation

Current flow through a PN junction diode under forward or reverse bias is governed by Shockley's equation:

I = I_s * ( exp( V / (n * V_T) ) - 1 )

Where $I_s$ is reverse saturation current, $n$ is ideality factor (1 for Ge, 2 for Si), and $V_T = \frac{k \cdot T}{q} \approx 26\text{mV}$ at $300\text{K}$.

Temperature Effects

  • Reverse Saturation Current ($I_s$): Doubles for every 10°C temperature rise due to thermal carrier generation.
  • Knee Voltage ($V_\gamma$): Drops by approximately $-2.5\text{mV/°C}$.

Transition ($C_T$) vs. Diffusion ($C_D$) Capacitance

Transition Capacitance ($C_T$): Dominates in reverse bias due to the charge separation in the depletion layer acting like a parallel plate capacitor ($C_T = \frac{\varepsilon \cdot A}{W}$).

Diffusion Capacitance ($C_D$): Dominates in forward bias due to stored minority carriers injected across the junction.

Clippers & Clampers

Clippers: Slices off voltage levels above or below selected reference points without altering the remaining signal shape.

Clampers: Adds a DC bias level to an AC waveform while keeping peak-to-peak amplitude constant.

Analog Unit II

Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT)

BJT Biasing & Thermal Stability

I_E = I_B + I_C | I_C = β * I_B + (1 + β) * I_CBO

Thermal Runaway: Self-heating raises $I_C$, elevating power dissipation ($P_D = V_{CE} \cdot I_C$), which increases junction temperature further in a destructive feedback loop.

Stability Factor ($S$): Quantifies sensitivity of $I_C$ to $I_{CBO}$: $S = \frac{\partial I_C}{\partial I_{CBO}}$. Minimal $S$ values are achieved using voltage-divider bias with emitter degeneration ($R_E$).

h-Parameters & Miller's Theorem

v_1 = h_i * i_1 + h_r * v_2 | i_2 = h_f * i_1 + h_o * v_2

Miller's Theorem: Converts feedback impedance $Z$ between input and output nodes into parallel equivalents: $Z_{in} = \frac{Z}{1 - A_v}$, $Z_{out} = \frac{Z}{1 - 1/A_v}$.

Analog Unit III

Field Effect Transistors (FET)

JFET & MOSFET Dynamics

Shockley Equation (JFET): I_D = I_DSS * ( 1 - V_GS / V_P )²

MOSFETs: Divided into Enhancement (EMOSFET) and Depletion (DMOSFET) types. They offer high input impedance ($10^{12}\,\Omega$) due to the insulated silicon dioxide ($\text{SiO}_2$) gate barrier.

Analog Unit IV

High-Frequency Transistor Analysis

Hybrid-π Model & Unity Gain Bandwidth

At high frequencies, internal parasitic junction capacitances ($C_{b'e}, C_{b'c}$) degrade gain.

Unity-Gain Frequency (f_T): f_T = g_m / ( 2 * π * (C_b'e + C_b'c) )
Analog Unit V

Negative Resistance Devices

Tunnel Diode & UJT Mechanics

Tunnel Diode: Ultra-heavily doped junction exhibiting quantum tunneling and negative differential resistance ($\frac{dV}{dI} < 0$).

Unijunction Transistor (UJT): Characterized by intrinsic standoff ratio $\eta = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + R_{B2}}$. Used in relaxation oscillators and SCR triggering.

Digital Unit I

Boolean Optimisation

K-Maps & Don't Care Conditions

Karnaugh Maps: Visual grouping method arranged in Gray code order to minimize Sum-of-Products (SOP) and Product-of-Sums (POS) Boolean functions.

Don't Care Terms (X): Invalid or unused input states leveraged during K-map grouping to maximize logic minimization.

Digital Unit II

Combinational Logic Circuits

Adders, Multiplexers & PLDs

Memoryless Circuits: Outputs depend solely on present inputs.

  • Adders: Half Adder, Full Adder, Carry Lookahead Adder (reduces propagation delay).
  • Data Routing: Multiplexers (Data Selectors) and Demultiplexers.
  • Programmable Logic: PLA (Programmable AND / Programmable OR) vs. PAL (Programmable AND / Fixed OR).
Digital Unit III

Sequential Logic Circuits

Flip-Flops, Registers & Counters

Flip-Flops: SR, D, JK, T. Race-around condition in JK ($J=K=1$ when pulse width $t_p > \Delta t$) is solved via Master-Slave or Edge Triggering.

Counters: Asynchronous (Ripple) vs. Synchronous (common clock source).

Digital Unit IV

Digital Logic Families

TTL vs. CMOS Parameters

  • Noise Margin: $NM_H = V_{OH(min)} - V_{IH(min)}$, $NM_L = V_{IL(max)} - V_{OL(max)}$.
  • TTL vs. CMOS: TTL is fast with high current drive; CMOS provides ultra-low static power draw and higher integration density.
Digital Unit V

Verilog HDL Architecture

Abstraction Levels in Verilog

  • Gate-Level: Direct instantiation of hardware logic gates (`and`, `or`, `nand`).
  • Dataflow: Functional modeling using continuous assignment statements (`assign out = a & b;`).
  • Behavioral: Algorithmic modeling inside procedural blocks (`always @(posedge clk)`).
Year 2 & 3 Track

Signals, Systems & DSP

Signal Classification & Transformations

Nyquist-Shannon Sampling Theorem: To prevent aliasing, a continuous signal must be sampled at a rate $f_s \ge 2 \cdot f_{max}$.

Continuous Fourier Transform: X(f) = ∫ x(t) * e^(-j * 2π * f * t) dt

Z-Transform: Discrete-time counterpart of Laplace Transform used for digital filter design (IIR & FIR filters).

Year 3 Track

Microprocessors & Embedded Systems

8085 / 8086 & ARM Architecture

8085 Architecture: 8-bit accumulator-based MPU with 16-bit address bus ($64\,\text{KB}$ memory access space).

RISC vs. CISC: Reduced Instruction Set Computing (ARM) utilizes pipelining and single-cycle execution vs. Complex Instruction Set Computing (x86).

Year 3 Track

Communication Engineering

Analog & Digital Modulation Scheme

Analog Modulation: Amplitude Modulation (AM), Frequency Modulation (FM), and Phase Modulation (PM).

Digital Modulation: ASK, FSK, PSK, and Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM) for high-bandwidth spectral efficiency.

Year 4 Advanced

VLSI Design & Semiconductor Fabrication

CMOS Inverter Dynamics & Fabrication Steps

CMOS Inverter: Consists of a PMOS pull-up network and an NMOS pull-down network. Exhibits near-zero static power dissipation.

Photolithography: Process of transferring geometric circuit shapes onto silicon wafers using UV light exposure, photoresist polymers, and chemical etching.
